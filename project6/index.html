Maps in Use

The Title – Reading the title is obviously a key part of understanding a map, but too often it’s overlooked when glancing at a page of new geographic information. The title will give you insight into the underlying purpose of the map’s creation and which information will be emphasized.

Compass – Even if the author is confident the reader will know which way is up, including a directional reference is customary. These designs can range from an elegant compass rose to a simple north arrow.

The Scale Bar – On maps that show a smaller area, the scale bar becomes very important to be able to tell how large certain features on the map are. A scale shows the relationship between the distance on a map and the distance on Earth. Without a scale, the reader can become disoriented. The size of land features in an unknown region is almost impossible to discern without a scale.

The Legend – The legend, or key, is an inset in the map that depicts the symbols and/or colors being used in the map and what they represent.

Lines of Latitude and Longitude – The vertical and horizontal lines running across maps are called lines of latitude and longitude, respectively. These allow you to record locations of specific spots along the Earth’s surface.
